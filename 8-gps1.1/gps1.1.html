What is Pair Programming?

Pair programing is a way for two people to work together on one problem on one computer. Each person has a defined role: one person navigates and provides instructions and the other drives and follows the instructions.

Why is Pair Programming Important to DBC?

  • Pair programming is a good way to catch bugs and prevent them from occurring
  • With two people working on a problem it's easier to solve it because you have more ideas
  • It is also a good way to learn from one another and to share knowledge about a code base

Driver vs Navigator

  • The navigator will provide instructions and help navigate the way around solving a problem
  • The driver will do the typing and implementing the instructions that the navigator provides

Reflection

Pairing for the first time was both a lot of fun and very productive. Here's quick rundown of what worked and what didn't:

What worked:
  • Having at least one person experienced in pairing. This prevents the awkward fumbling that occurs when neither party quite knows what to do.
  • Having roles so that one person doesn't have to focus on typing and can catch typos and syntax errors
What didn't work so well
  • The only thing that didn't work is having a low familiarity with the tools we were using slowed us down and prevented us from making the full use of the time

Overall I think pairing has a lot of benefits and its definitely a method I would use in future.
